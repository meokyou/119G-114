    National Museum of Civil Rights

    The National Civil Rights Museum is a complex of museums and historic buildings in Memphis, Tennessee; its exhibits trace the history of the civil rights movement in the United States from the 17th century to the present.

    Connection to Invisible Man

    The museum is a chronicle of African American history and is a representation of the abuses wrongfully subjected onto them. It shows some of the real life parallels of the racially motivated events in Invisible Man. It provides context to the settings of 1970, America and allows us to better navigate the social landscape the narrator is challenged with. The subject-matter of the hotel is quite broad and provides large quantites of information to the general condition of Black people. The events in the museum lead up to the books time-setting.

Roy Bryant, husband of the mistress, counselled by his half-brother, John William Milam, assisted by J.W. Threatening the old man, then at age 64, Mister Wright did not resist his abduction. Despite those closest to him looking for him rigorously they—sadly—could nor would find him alive again. Three days after his, gruesome, unjustified, murder by way of Bryant's entourage of hirees, Till's swollen and disgigured corpse was found by two boys who were fishin in the Tallahctchie River.
